Learning Labs

Preparing students for careers in
San Diego's Priority Sectors


Our four labs simulate work environments and real-world skills application in Tech, Advanced Manufacturing, and Health & Biotech - recession resilient sectors projected to grow in wages and job openings in our regional economy.

The Labs

Advanced Manufacturing & Engineering

Students will explore the roles, occupations, machines, and technologies related to advanced manufacturing. They will design a product using engineering software, such as AutoCAD or Solidworks, before receiving hands-on training in professional manufacturing equipment, including 3D printers, laser cutters, hydraulics and pneumatics, welding simulators, and more. The students then use these tools to create their product.


Health & Biotech

Students will learn technical skills and work together to complete a case challenge based on a real-world health problem. Students assume roles in the health sciences field, such as doctors, hospital administrators, lab researchers, and pharmaceutical developers and use professional lab equipment and collect data to solve the case challenge.


Information & Communication Technology

Students encounter several simulations that require them to apply foundational knowledge and develop a technology solution. Activities will cover the diverse careers in tech: from setting up server hardware and connecting network cables to build a functioning server to learning an Apple programming language and developing an iPad app. Participants will even learn how to pilot drones and take aerial photos, the images from which they will use to create a VR/AR experience.

Middle School STEAM

Featuring 3D printers, laser cutters, CNC mills, arduinos, raspberry pis, and more, students receive instruction in and experiment with numerous emerging technologies. Participants rotate between modules and stations to learn different STEAM skills and then apply these skills to solve a problem. The Middle School STEAM Lab also serves as a testing ground for teachers and schools investigating how to set up a makerspace on their campuses.

Additional Information

Pre-Lab Preparation

Before arrival, students will explore their Lab's industry by completing career assessments to identify their strengths and discover careers that align to their skills and interests. 

Time Commitment

Each Lab will have 4 week-long sessions this summer from July 12th - August 6th. Participants are expected to attend every day of their lab week, Monday to Friday from 8 am - 1 pm.

What do I need to bring?

Each Lab includes the necessary equipment for students to complete the activities. All we ask is that students bring a positive attitude. Lunch each day will be provided!

Connections to Employers

Each Lab features San Diego employers from that field to provide career advice and guide students to the roles, skillsets, and organizations that most interest them based on their Lab experience.

The Space
