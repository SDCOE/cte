College and Career Readiness

Being college and career ready means that you graduate from high school with a career goal and a plan for how to get there. Scroll down to find resources that will help you get started determining your interests and learning about the jobs available in the San Diego economy...

1

Get work experience.

Use the Program Finder to find CTE pathway programs in San Diego high schools or check out our work-based learning board to connect with employers in your field of interest.

2

What problems do you want to solve?

Take a Career Assessment to determine which occupations align with your skills and interests and what types of work environments will empower you to succeed.

3

Explore careers.

Use the Labor Market resources below to learn about the diverse industry sectors that comprise San Diego's economy and the skills that employers need.

4

Education and Training

Ensure you're prepared to graduate college and know the types and levels of education required for various occupations. Also, find scholarship opportunities to help fund your training.

WORK BASED LEARNING DASHBOARD

Work-based learning (WBL) activities connect you with employers to learn about careers you're interested in. WBL can be presentations by professionals about their work, tours of a work site, internships, jobs, and more. Check out our current opportunities, including hiring events!

Note: many of the virtual activities will be recorded, so if you see something you like but can't make, register anyway so that you're on the mailing list for when they send out the recording.

Note: Be sure to check the scholarship due date before applying!

SCHOLARSHIP DASHBOARD

Many companies and foundations offer funds to help students pay for college, certificate programs, and other professional training after high school. Review our list of currently available scholarships to determine which you are eligible to apply for. We update the list several times throughout the year.
