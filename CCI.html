College/Career Readiness Tools and Resources

Impact college and career readiness at your school

The College/Career Indicator is one of the metrics from the California School Dashboard, measuring student preparedness for college and career.

What is the California School Dashboard?

The California School Dashboard provides parents and educators with meaningful information on school and district progress so they can participate in decisions to improve student learning. It is part of the accountability and continuous improvement system meant to ensure schools are meeting the needs of California's diverse student population.

Learn more about the California School Dashboard and System of Support. 

What is the College and Career Readiness Indicator (CCI)? 

This indicator takes into account a number of components students may meet to be college and career ready. Students who are considered "prepared" meet specific combinations of these components.

Take a closer look at each component with our video series below!

It's imporant to note that the state board of education revises these metrics regularly, and the resources here are up to date as of December 2020. 

The effect on this indicator due to changes in state testing due to the COVID-19 Pandemic are not yet known, however you can find more details related to the COVID-19 coronaviruas and accountability here. 

An overview of the CCI

CTE Pathway Completion 

Students must complete a pathway (2 or more sequence courses in the same sector) with a grade of C- or better in the capstone course.

In addition students must meet one of the following: scoring 3/2 on the SBAC, earn college credit, score 3+ on an AP exam or a 4+ on an IB exam, or complete UC/CSU a-g requirements.

Completing UC/CSU a-g requirements

Students must complete the minimum admission requirements set out by the UC/CSU systems, consisting of 15 yearlong courses across disciplines. 

In addition students must meet one of the following: CTE pathway completion, scoring 3/2 on the SBAC, earn college credit, or score 3+ on an AP exam or a 4+ on an IB exam.

State Seal of Biliteracy

​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​The State Seal of Biliteracy is an award given by a school, district, or county office of education to high school graduates who have attained a high level of proficiency in speaking, reading and writing in one or more languages in addition to English.

This component is only available in districts with board approved programs. Learn more about the California State Seal of Biliteracy and which districts offer this seal at the SDCOE State Seal of Biliteracy page.
