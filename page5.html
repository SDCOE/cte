The Competition

Teams

Teams consist of 4 students (+1 alternate) from San Diego County high schools, and 1 instructor sponsor.

Judges

There will be a panel of 3 judges representing some of the top restaurants in San Diego, who will declare 1 winner.

Prizes

Participants receive a tour and gift certificate to one of San Diego's renowned restaurants as well as an informational interview with a professional chef.

The Rules

Teams will be provided a list of ingredients prior to the competition from which they will design and prepare an appetizer and entree in 1 hour. 

Ingredients and Menu

The 2 courses combined must contain the following components: a protein, 4 fruits and/or vegetables, 2 grains, spices and garnishes. Teams may bring a pre-made sauce to enhance dishes. 

Start Time

Teams will start in 5-10 minute intervals to stagger the food presentations. We will draw to determine the order, but you will be allowed to change time
slots if needed.

Equipment

Teams will be provided 2 stove burners, an oven, pans, knives, cutting boards, spatulas, plates, thermometers, fridge space, ice, and a prep table. Teams may bring additional utensils. Ovens will be preheated.

Judging

Judges will use a scorecard to evaluate each course. 2 dishes will be prepared for each course: 1 for the judges and 1 to display. Teams will be provided the scorecard before the competition to help them design their menu.

The Space

Master Chefs 2020 will be hosted by Southwest High School in their beautiful culinary arts space.

10 Food Preparation Stations

Each station features 2 stove burners, 1 oven, 1 large stainless steel preparation table, cooking pans and spatulas, cutting boards and knives, thermometers, and display plates. Each team will also be provided fridge space, ice, a sanitation bucket, and trash can.

1 Instructional Workspace

At the front of the classroom is an instructional workspace with a stove, food preparation area, and an overhead camera which displays live video on a screen facing the teams.
